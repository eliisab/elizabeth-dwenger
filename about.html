About

Hi! I am an Artifical Intelligence student with a passion for design and photography based in Amsterdam. I craft digital experiences to delight, educate, and inspire people, with the aim of making a positive difference to their lives through design and storytelling.

I'm 21 years old, and I live in Amsterdam, Netherlands! I grew up mostly in Minneapolis, Minnesota, and now I attend Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. I originally was going to study computer science, but I switched to Artificial Intelligence after about a month (long story which involves listening to the podcast, "The End Of The World with Josh Clark", but not a particularly interesting story). I spend a lot of my time studying, editing, taking photos, & working from cafes. A fun fact about me is that I actually hate shopping; however, if I ever do "shop" (or more correctly put, online "window shopping"), I really like shoes (I'm kind of a sneakerhead). Additionally, I love learning about & discovering different cultures. My favorite thing to photograph is people around the world, doing everyday things. I'm a total foodie, and I've come to terms with it. I will spend any dollar amount on a good food experience. Despite Amsterdam being an ungodly expensive city, I probably still spend most of my money on food. I love music, particularly reggaeton and that of the hip-hop/R&B nature. Despite being in a technical field, I love being outdoors. Nature does this thing to me, where it just kind of . . . I don't know, takes my breath away & makes me feel super at home. This is especially counter-intuitive because I am the biggest city girl, but still, if I am not in a hurry, I am known to walk where I need to go just so that I can spend time outdoors. I just try to live an exciting, genuine, and happy life. I'm a SUCH yes girl. I say yes to a lot of things because life's short and we get to make it exactly what we want while we're here. so why not do all the things.

Photography and artificial intelligence may seem like two very unrelated interests, but I think each complements the other. Bias within AI is fascinating to me, and I think it also gives an insight into why people hold certain ideas. Not to get too Orwellian, but the idea that each person is trained on a "dataset" throughout their lives to form the opinions they hold is an idea I find fascinating. Photography, on the other hand, can show the results of these ideas. Capturing the irreplaceable moments of one's life is a tangible outcome of every decision and action one has taken in one's life. The best part to me is that no matter how much time passes, one can look back and feel just like it did on that day.

You can reach me by email. Shoot me a message and let's make some magic :)

xx

elizabeth
